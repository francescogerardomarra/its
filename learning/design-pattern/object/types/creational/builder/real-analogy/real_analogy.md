# Real Life Analogy

Imagine you walk into a **car factory** to order a **custom car**.
You don’t build the car yourself, instead, you tell a **car builder** what features you want:

* the brand and model (e.g., Bugatti, Lamborghini);
* the color and number of doors;
* the type of screen and interior design;
* optional features like a sunroof or sport wheels.

The **builder** takes these specifications and follows a **step-by-step construction process** to assemble the car. You, as the client, don’t have to worry about *how* the car is assembled or which parts go in first, you just describe what you want.

Sometimes, the factory employs a **director**, this is like a **production manager** who knows the exact sequence of steps needed to build certain standard models.

---

**For example:**

* the “Bugatti director” knows the standard order to build a Bugatti configuration.
* the “Lamborghini director” knows the specific steps for a Lamborghini.

Both directors can use different builders, one might produce a **real car**, and another might produce a **digital 3D model (schema)** of the same car, but they follow the **same building instructions**.

---

**This separation of roles allows:**

* clients to request complex products easily (without knowing technical details);
* builders to focus on *how* to assemble the product;
* directors to standardize *what sequence* of steps to follow.
